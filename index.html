“And what about those [writers' workshop] critiques, by the way? How valuable are they? Not very, in my experience, sorry. A lot of them are maddeningly vague. I love the feeling of Peter's story, someone may say. It had something... a sense of I don't know... there's a loving kind of you know... I can't exactly describe it....

It seems to occur to few of the attendees that if you have a feeling you just can't describe, you might just be, I don't know, kind of like, my sense of it is, maybe in the wrong fucking class.”

― Stephen King, On Writing: A Memoir of the Craft

Denver Story Collective is an intentionally small, bi-weekly critique group that strives to create actual, useful feedback. We focus on helping members develop their craft and voice with focused, in-person critique.

It is also a growing list of thoughtfully constructed and well-critiqued pieces of writing grown straight from the Denver community. Below you'll find some of Denver's best written work, oral stories, and games.

The Critique Group.

The DSC is not a place to circle-jerk with other artists, or pontificate about your experience. No one gives a shit where you’ve been published, how many Twitter followers you have, or how long you’ve been writing. All we care about is how good your story is - and how good it could be.

To become a part of DSC, join the meetup - we'll ask you for a writing sample to see if you're a good match for our current critique group. Lots more information is available at the Meetup Page.

The DSC is run by Nick Anderson, a Denver-based writer. His work has appeared in Nerve, Fried, and Paste Magazine.
